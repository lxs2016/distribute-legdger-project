5.4 Design Constraints for the Betting Mechanism
Because the betting and payout mechanism is open-ended, you must explicitly document and justify

1.Solvency (for your chosen rewards):
4•Your contract must be able to pay all promised rewards implied by your mechanism at settle-

6 Security and Fairness Requirements
6.1 High-Level Requirement
Your Stage 1 and 2 contract should besecure,gas efficient, andfair. You should explicitly
design for adversarial players.
6.2 Code-Level Security (Non-Exhaustive Minimum Set)
Your implementation must explicitly consider and mitigate, where applicable:
•Reentrancyrisks in any path that transfers ETH or interacts with external contracts (including
token transfers, if your design involves external callbacks or untrusted recipients).
•DoS with revert(e.g., failed transfers causing the game to become unfinishable).
•State machine integrity:illegal transitions, repeated settlement, double-claiming rewards,
replay across games.
•Access control:only intended parties can call sensitive functions in sensitive states (including
any administrative/pool-management functionality, if present).
•Funds safety:no permanent lock of deposits/rewards; refunds and settlement must remain
possible under adversarial behavior.
•Input validation:address sanity checks, zero-value edge cases, overflow/underflow assumptions
(even with Solidity 0.8.x safety).
6.3 Mechanism-Level Security and Fairness (Non-Exhaustive Minimum Set)
Your protocol must explicitly address:
•Preventing regret/abort (“I refuse to continue after learning I’m likely to lose”):
5–You must include enforceable deadlines/timeouts and an explicit consequence (e.g., default
loss, refund scheme, or slash) for non-cooperation.
•Randomness manipulation resistance:
–You must explain your randomness mechanism and why no single party can deterministically
force a desired outcome.
–A naive single-source randomness design (e.g., solely using one party’s input or solely using a
miner-influenced field) is not acceptable without strong justification.
•Front-running / information leakage considerations:
–If any step reveals information that enables the other party to selectively proceed, you must
mitigate and/or justify.
•Gas fairness:
–You must consider whether one party systematically pays more gas than the other, and justify
or mitigate.
•Token bonus pool integrity:
–You must address how the token bonus pool can be drained (e.g., repeated tiny games, Sybil
players, jackpot-style exploitation), and what design choices prevent or reduce such abuse.
–You must specify and justify how pool insufficiency is handled without breaking correctness
or fairness.

You are encouraged to use AI tools for:
•Framework & Architecture Design:Brainstorming state machine flows, discussing potential
betting mechanisms, or understanding how to design secure randomness.
•Debugging:pasting specific error messages or reverting code snippets to identify the root cause
of a bug.
•Syntax & Refactoring:Asking for help with Solidity syntax specifics, optimizing gas in a
specific function, or formatting code.
•Security Auditing:Asking an AI to review a specific block of code for common vulnerabilities
(e.g., reentrancy, integer overflow).

the original content is yours).

you submitted your work.

Security & Fairness analysis) from scratch. The reasoning must be your own.
